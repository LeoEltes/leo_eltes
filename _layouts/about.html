Swedish producer and composer Leo Eltes studies Music Production at Linnaeus University in Sweden. His love for film and music has made his approach to scoring natural and straight forward. Where he finds no shame in using the same two chords, as long as they express what the film maker wants to express.


Most of his inspiration regarding sound design comes from minimalistic synth producers and artists such as Trent Reznor (from Nine Inch Nails), Nils Frahm, Mike Lindsay, James Blake, Bon Iver and Grouper. But he takes great melodic and emotional inspiration from artists with pioneering voices like Lorde, Joni Mitchell, Arcade Fire and Bj√∂rk. With these contemporaries in mind his music has a minimalistic approach where less is more. Letting the observer or listener process and decide what is important.


Alongside the love for music and film, he has a passion for programming and exploring the combination and expressiveness of programming and sound put together. He programmed visuals to some of the tracks from his recently produced EP, Bedroom Productions (you may watch these by clicking on Visuals in the menu bar). Some of these visuals are interactive and dependant on the user's action as well as the frequencies the song journeys through.


Developing and communicating ideas are a big part of writing and producing music, whether it is for film or for artists. As a participant in SR's (Sveriges Radio, the public radio of Sweden) Creative Lab 2018, as well as a composer for his brother's video documentation of their home town Kungsbacka, he has explored how to evaluate and review ideas from different angles.


He is also unquestionably professional at taking care of and recording cats.
